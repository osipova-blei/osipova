Ekaterina Osipova

Hello. My name is Ekaterina and I am currently a Master's student of science and technology studies (STS) at the University of Vienna.

I am interested in a range of topics focusing on gender and sexuality and their intersections with science and technology, queer and trans health, public engagement with techno-science as well as hacking and tinkering. I am particularly fond of post-actor-network-theories like ontological politics, assemblage thinking, and care in/for techno-science.

Currently I am writing my master's thesis on trans people's perspectives on polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS) treatment, their experiences with the medical system, as well as their envisionings of an inclusive and attentive PCOS care.
Also I am a research assistant at the Department of Science and Technology Studies at the University of Vienna.

Current Projects

  • In collaboration with Azadeh Badieijaryani and Katta Spiel at TU Wien we are researching the enactments of and technosexual scripts inscribed into teledildonic sex toys. By focusing on a particular teledildonics company we explore how their products' marketing and materiality is (re)scripting intimacy and technosexual utopias.
